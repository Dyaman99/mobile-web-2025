Addressing the Concerns/Issues

How I fixed some style issues and errors

After getting feedback from these people, I tried my best to address all the issues. I made most of the links clickable by adding links to the sections on the page, and I also added a location page, so now when a user clicks the location icon, it takes them to a new page with an embedded map. I changed the colors of the articles in the special-collection section to match the site's theme more. I replaced all the image placeholders with actual images that are optimized for the website. I fixed the signup field using additional CSS. Furthermore, I styled the hyperlinks on the articles in furniture categories and special collection sections. They now look more like buttons. I also edited my "shop the sale" slides and made it an infinite loop, and turned on the autoplay. There was another bug in the location icon on the nav bar, and I was able to fix it with some effort. It did not go to the very left side of the nav bar, near the shopping cart icon, like I planned. I figured that the location icon was part of the flex that controls all of the navigation and was evenly spaced alongside all the other elements of the nav bar, such as the logo and the hamburger menu. I fixed it by using flexbox: I made the two icons (location and shopping cart) another flexbox inside the main flex, so now they were attached.
